Overview

Ensure Cure.fit platform has enough good quality supply of partners there is a need to follow stringent partner identification and verification process before they are onboarded onto our platform.

Currently the entire process is managed via Zoho CRM. While this streamlines the backend process, partner facing experience is still broken as they get multiple communication through various touch points without giving a coherent onboarding flow.

Problem Statement

Creating a single streamline process for users to onboard on cure.fit marketplace as a service provider.

Goals / Key success metrics:
  1. Turn around time(TAT) from 15-18 to 7-9 days for Fitness partners and from 7-9 to 2~3 days for Health Partners.
  2. Reduce lead management team size from 21 to 3~5 members.

Deep Analysis of issues

User observations and feedback sessions helped us collect a lot of qualitative data. All this data helped us list down the major pain points and go further about it.

1. No clear starting point or adheration to the discussion flow

As the portal was built focusssing on the managing aspect of OKRs it provides no set way in which OKR's can be analysed or discussed upon. The only way of checking certain projects is through filtering and with less time in your hand it becomes really difficult for users to go this way.

2. Back and forth as information is placed across the portal

There is no way to check project KRs and individual KRs at the same time for a project which increases a lot of difficulties as users have to keep moving back and forth between the pages.

3. Discussion driving status colors- hidden

Status colors drive the complete discussion, green KRs are not discussed and just skimmed but red and critical KRs are discussed in depth to understand what went wrong and what needs to be done now.

4. Cognition leading to delays and deviation

Difficulty in consumption of data due a lot of information and actions which are meant more for managing the OKRs but not required during the quarterly discussion.

Analysing user needs

1. Easy way to check all important Projects and KR statuses without fighting with a lot of other information and actions. A better alternative of the manaement view and google sheets.

2. Keeping the status colors as the primary carrier of the discussion to avoid any deviation.

Defining scope of the project

Using data visualiztion to provide a better means of navigation through the projects and KR’s for all OKR related discussions and meetings taking OKR status as a primary deciding and discussion factor.

Future Scope:Adding more and more data visualization tools to gain more insights with less effort.

Further Iterations

User feedbacks were satisfacory, people were aligned with the overall concept and most of them found it really helpful. There wer a few feedbacks on UI and scroll which were again iterated and optimized.

Key Learnings

  1. Users are unpredictable: Users never behave the way we expect. As a designer we should always be ready for unpredicatable responses and ierating accroding to them..
  2. Alpha testing can be biased: Testing within the team and stakeholders can be biased and may/may not provide actual refelection. It is always better to Beta test on real users in real scenarios.
