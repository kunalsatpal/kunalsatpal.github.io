What is OKR?

OKR (Objective Key Results) is a goal system used by many organizations for tracking progress in terms of objectives and results that are to be achieved.

At Cure.fit top to bottom approach is followrd for setting up OKRs. Which means that there are certain company level objectives linked to which some project level OKRs and again linked to that there are several other OKRs to the very individual level.

OKRs help in providing direction to the work, making sure everyone in the organization is working towards the company's goals as well creating a sense of ownership of your individual key results. OKRs are written in a particular set pattern recorording the exact objective and what key result are we trying to achieve and how we are trcaking it. Hence the name Objective Key Resukts.

How are OKRs tracked?

OKRs are tracked and reviewed at mid and end of every quarter using color codes. This helps is understanding the overall requirements and also in making sure that proper attention can be given to the required objectives.

Separate OKR discussion Discussions are scheduled for every vertical in the organisation with the co-founders for this. Where all what went wrong, right and further plan is discussed.

Quarterly OKR Status Discussion

Status of OKRs is discussed at the end of every quarter with the co-founders and futher stakeholders of the company, presented by the respective vertical team. This is a crucial meeting as futher plan and startergy is discussed here. Being a super important meeting with really busy people, it is essential to utilize every second of the meeting. And that's where the problem starts:

How does this delay impact?
  1. Time is value and in startups time is money, extended discussion lead to a lot of disruptions in further schedules. Being a larger hall meeting it is always difficult to schedule a follow up advesly impacting and delaying important decisions.
  2. Even if the meeting is scheduled it is not possible to recollect everything properly and a further context is required. It affects the further planning of allocating resources and bandwidth to the places which might need them.
  3. It creates an overall grey area affecting a lot of decision making around other things in the organisation.
But why is this delay happening?

For understanding the main reason for this delay it's important for us to understand our users and their goals first.

Understanding our users

The exact user behaviour in a quaterly meeting cannot be replicated in a usability sessions thus user observation during quarterly meetings and organically feedbacks collected across the organisation was the primary mode of research for the project.

Our User goals:
  1. Make sure everything is on the right track in a project. Focus on the key highlights and check the progress there.
  2. Check the reason if any project is falling back and also discuss future plans for that. How things can be improved or if more resources are required.
  3. Discuss next quarter goals according to the current progress and make sure the are inline with the company goals.
Deep Analysis of pain points from research

User observations and feedback sessions helped us collect a lot of qualitative data. All this data helped us list down the major pain points and go further about it.

1. No clear starting point or adheration to the discussion flow

As the portal was built focusssing on the managing aspect of OKRs it provides no set way in which OKR's can be analysed or discussed upon. The only way of checking certain projects is through filtering and with less time in your hand it becomes really difficult for users to go this way.

2. Lot of back and forth as information is placed across the portal

There is no way to check project KRs and individual KRs at the same time for a project which increases a lot of difficulties as users have to keep moving back and forth between the pages. A lot of times people skip checking all the information because of this.

3. Discussion driving status colors- hidden

Status colors drive the complete discussion, green KRs are not discussed and just skimmed but red and critical KRs are discussed in depth to understand what went wrong and what needs to be done now.

4. Cognition leading to delays and deviation

Difficulty in consumption of data due a lot of information and actions which are meant more for managing the OKRs but not required during the quarterly discussion. Having a lot of information upfront makes it difficult to focus on particulars in the meeting, lot of the time people are not intersted in the complete story from green KR's and only want to discuss what went wrong. Moreover a board of 10 people is easily deviated by slightest of distraction.

Analysing user needs

1. Easy way to check all important Projects and KR statuses without fighting with a lot of other information and actions. A better alternative of the manaement view and google sheets.

2. Keeping the status colors as the primary carrier of the discussion to avoid any deviation.

Defining scope of the project

Using data visualiztion to provide a better means of navigation through the projects and KR’s for all OKR related discussions and meetings taking OKR status as a primary deciding and discussion factor.

Future Scope:Adding more and more data visualization tools to gain more insights with less effort.
