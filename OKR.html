What is OKR?

OKR (Objective Key Results) is a goal system used by many organizations for tracking progress in terms of objectives and results that are to be achieved.

At Cure.fit top to bottom approach is followrd for setting up OKRs. Which means that there are certain company level objectives linked to which some project level OKRs and again linked to that there are several other OKRs to the very individual level.

OKRs help in providing direction to the work, making sure everyone in the organization is working towards the company's goals as well creating a sense of ownership of your individual key results.

How are OKRs tracked?

OKRs are tracked and reviewed at mid and end of every quarter using color codes. This helps is understanding the overall requirements and also in making sure that proper attention can be given to the required objectives.

Separate OKR discussion Discussions are scheduled for every vertical in the organisation with the co-founders for this.

Quarterly OKR Status Discussion

Status of OKRs is discussed at the end of every quarter with the co-founders of the company, presented by the respective vertical team.

How does this delay impact?
  1. Time is value and in startups time is money extended Discussion lead to a lot of disruptions in further schedules
  2. It affects the further planning of allocating resources and bandwidth to the places which might need them.
  3. It creates an overall grey area affecting a lot of decision making.
But why is this delay happening?

For understanding the main reason for this delay it's important for us to understand our user goals first.

Understanding user goals first

User observations and user feedback accross the board was the primiary mode for collecting insights.

User goals:
  1. Make sure everything is on the right track in a project
  2. Check the reason if any project is falling back and also discuss future plans for that.
  3. Discuss next quarter goals
More Insights -
1. A lot of back and forth in the discussion as information is placed across the portal

There is no way to check project KRs and individual KRs at the same time for a project which increases a lot of difficulties as users have to keep moving back and forth between the pages.

2. No adheration to the user requirement and discussion flow

As the portal was built focusssing on the managing aspect of OKRs it provides no set way in which OKR's can be analysed or discussed upon. The only way of checking certain projects is through filtering.

3. Cognition leading to delays and deviation

Difficulty in consumption of data due to lack of hierarchy and a lot of information and actions which are meant more for managing the OKRs but not required during the quarterly discussion.

4. Discussion driving status colors are hidden

Status colors drive the complete discussion, green KRs are not discussed and just skimmed but red and critical KRs are discussed in depth to understand what went wrong and what needs to be done now.

Defining scope of the project

Providing a better means of navigation through the projects and KR’s for all OKR related discussions and meetings taking OKR status as a primary deciding and discussion factor.

Future Scope: Using data visualization to pace up the process and provide better insights.
